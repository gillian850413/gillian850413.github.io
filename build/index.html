The Gap Between Billionaires and Us

How great is the gap between billionaires and the citizens?

By: Hung Yi Lin, Jingxian Na, Pin Huey Chiang, Siyu Liu

There are a lot of people who are eager to become a billionaire. They want to be wealthy and devote their abilities to make a better world. The Forbes’ billionaire list collects the top-ranking billionaires around the world. If you want to become a rich person, you may want to know how much you need to earn to achieve your dream. This raises some research questions:

  • Which region has the highest possibility to become a billionaire?
  • If you were a normal citizen, what is the income gap between the billionaire and you?

Dataset Description

This dataset collects the Forbes’ Billionaire list in 1996, 2001, and 2014, which includes information such as billionaire name, year company was founded, net worth in billions, and so on. It also includes the country GDP, region and industry for further comparisons. This project provides a preliminary analysis of the billionaire dataset and focuses on wealth distribution, wealth differences, GDP among billionaires and citizens in different regions.

Number of Billionaires in each country

Let’s start with looking at where the Forbes billionaires were located in the world. The stacked bar chart shows the billionaire distribution of each country in 1996, 2011 and 2014. By grouping the countries into regions, each bar in the chart represents the aggregated net worth of each billionaire within each region in 1996, 2001, and 2014. GDP and Proportion trend

In order to obtain more insights regarding the billionaires and their regions, we make the barchart and discover the correlation between GDP and average net worth. The bar chart below shows us the GDP of seven regions in 1996, 2001 and 2014, while the scatterplots display the proportion of the numbers of billionaires held in the population. Conclusion

If you want to be a billionaire, the U.S. will be the best place to stay to pursue that goal since it has the largest proportion of billionaires. Also given the innovative and rapidly developing nature of US industries, the US also has the highest wealth differences and the absolute highest net worth among billionaires. However, the largest wealth inequality doesn’t happen in North America, rather it’s the South Asian region where the Riches are super rich while the Poors are extremely poor.
